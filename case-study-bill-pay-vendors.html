Square · Bill Pay · Vendor acceptance

Getting vendors to trust their first Bill Pay payment

Vendors were abandoning Bill Pay before payout. I led design across the notification email and payment portal, partnering with engineering and risk from day one to ship a compliant Plaid connection flow in four weeks.

Design DRI
Vendor acceptance · Bill Pay · Payments
4 weeks
PM · Engineering · Risk

Situation

  • Square Bill Pay vendors were receiving payment notifications but abandoning before completing bank setup
  • Vendor payment acceptance completion was at 52%. Sellers lacked confidence payments would actually land
  • The initial assumption was that the portal flow had too many inputs and lacked trust

Task

Redesign the vendor acceptance experience to increase completion.

I knew early this was not just a UX problem. It was a trust and compliance problem that required engineering and risk partners from the start.

Action

  • Before designing anything, brought engineering and risk into the problem framing. Not a handoff conversation, a definition conversation
  • We needed to answer together: what connection methods were technically feasible, and what compliance required around bank account access
  • That early conversation revealed the real constraint: we could not remove manual entry. It had to remain available as a compliant fallback even if we de-emphasized it
  • Knowing that upfront changed the design direction. Instead of eliminating options, I designed a clear primary path through Plaid while keeping manual entry accessible but non-default
  • Worked with engineering to understand Plaid integration requirements early enough that consent flow, messaging, and sequencing were shaped around what the API actually needed, not retrofitted after the fact
  • With a four week timeline, there was no room for late-stage surprises. Early alignment with engineering and risk was the only way to ship something compliant and conversion-optimized at the same time

Exploration: Discovery

Research surfaced three recurring concerns during setup: trust with sensitive information, friction finding bank details, and urgency to get paid without extra steps.

Sensitive information

“I want to get paid, but putting my bank info into a new payment flow makes me pause. That’s pretty sensitive stuff, so I want to know it’s safe before I enter anything.”

Jordan, Northside Ceramics Co.

Finding bank details

“I don’t always have my routing number and account number sitting around. If I have to stop what I’m doing and go find it, I might just come back later.”

Priya, Bloom Street Bakery

Speed and urgency

“When someone sends me money, I just want to get it and move on. I don’t want a bunch of extra steps between me and getting paid.”

Alex, Golden Hour Photography

The payment notification email was failing the same trust test. It was optimized for notification, not conversion. Vendors opened it asking: Is this real? Why am I getting this? What do I need to do? Is it safe?

I redesigned the hierarchy to lead with amount, sender, how to get paid, then details, not sender, amount, and metadata.

  • Notification email click-through improved from 69% to 87%
  • Vendor payment acceptance completion jumped from 52% to 73%
  • Manual bank entry dropped from 78% to 49%. Vendors chose the trusted path when it was clearly the default
