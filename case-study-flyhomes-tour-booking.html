Situation

During the 2020–2021 housing market boom, Flyhomes customers were competing in an extremely fast-moving real estate environment. Homes were often receiving multiple offers within days, meaning speed and access were critical to helping buyers succeed.

At the time, Flyhomes relied on a manual tour coordination process involving tour booking agents and on-the-ground tour specialists. This created operational bottlenecks that directly impacted customers:

  • Buyers waited hours for tour confirmations
  • Internal teams spent significant time manually coordinating schedules
  • Inefficient routing created delays and unnecessary operational costs
  • Customers were losing opportunities in a market where timing could determine whether they won a home

From a customer perspective, the stakes were high. Flyhomes buyers were touring an average of 40 homes before successfully getting an offer accepted, and we needed to dramatically reduce the amount of time and effort required to help them find and win their home.

Task

Lead the design strategy and experience for automating the tour booking workflow for Flyhomes’ internal teams.

The goal was to create a scalable internal tool that:

  • Reduced time from tour request to confirmation
  • Improved operational efficiency for booking teams
  • Helped buyers move faster in a competitive market
  • Reduced the number of homes customers needed to tour before finding success

Action

From workflow mapping to API integration and a ground-up redesign.

I started by mapping the existing tour coordination workflow using a Jobs-to-Be-Done framework to understand how booking teams managed requests, where decisions were being made, and where automation could remove friction.

Through this synthesis, I identified key workflow gaps:

  • Manual assignment of tour specialists
  • Lack of visibility into tour request status
  • Inefficient scheduling coordination with seller agents
  • Limited tools for managing high-volume requests

To address these gaps, I partnered with my product squad to evaluate API solutions and integrate external providers:

  • Skedulo — automated tour routing and agent assignment
  • ShowingTime — enabled access to seller agent availability and scheduling

With new API capabilities in place, I redesigned the internal tour management experience from the ground up. Throughout the process, I ran rapid feedback sessions with internal users to validate designs, identify workflow gaps, and iterate quickly.

Design

A tour table and detail experience focused on speed, prioritization, and operational clarity.

Before · Tour detail experience

The existing tour detail experience had several usability issues:

  • Limited hierarchy made critical information difficult to find
  • Inconsistent components created cognitive load
  • Excessive use of color reduced clarity in a B2B workflow
  • Repetitive information patterns slowed scanning
  • Accessibility issues impacted usability

After · Redesigned tour management

Key improvements included:

  • Redesigned navigation to create more space for high-volume tour management
  • Updated table columns based on feedback from booking managers and end users
  • Improved information hierarchy for faster decision-making
  • Added batch reassignment workflows to support operational changes like team outages or turnover
  • Improved accessibility across the experience

Results

The redesigned experience significantly improved the speed and efficiency of the tour booking process.

  • Reduced time from tour request received to confirmation from ~5 hours to under 30 minutes
  • Exceeded our original goal by reducing the average number of homes toured before an accepted offer from 40 homes to 18 homes
  • Reduced tour-related support calls by 44%
  • Created a scalable foundation for automating critical operational workflows

This project demonstrated how thoughtful internal tooling could directly impact customer outcomes. By reducing operational friction, we helped Flyhomes buyers move faster and compete more effectively during one of the most competitive housing markets in history.
