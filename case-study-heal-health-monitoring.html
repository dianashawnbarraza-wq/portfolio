Situation

Heal provided on-demand, same-day doctor house calls—bringing accessible care into patients’ homes rather than treating every visit as a one-off appointment.

Our ambition was primary, preventative care delivered by Heal doctors—not episodic urgent visits. As Heal moved toward Medicare acceptance, we saw an opportunity: proactive, data-driven monitoring could help doctors treat patients with chronic conditions at home, especially adults 65 and older who benefit most from continuous oversight between visits.

Doctors needed better visibility into patient health over time. Without it, chronic conditions were harder to manage remotely, repeat engagement suffered, and Heal could not fully demonstrate the preventative value Medicare required.

Task

Strategize and design an end-to-end patient health monitoring experience for physician use.

As Design DRI, I partnered across product, engineering, and clinical teams to define how monitoring would work for doctors recommending it, patients living with chronic conditions, and the business model behind recurring care.

We set a concrete growth target: increase Heal’s repeat patient rate from 39% to 45% by introducing patient monitoring—while building the clinical and product foundation needed for Medicare approval.

Action

Discovery with clinicians, then rapid prototyping with a standing doctor panel.

Discovery · Arriving at an ideal patient journey

I began with an interview with the Head of Medicine, then worked alongside a project manager to establish a doctor panel for weekly feedback sessions.

Together we answered two critical questions for our MVP: how and when a doctor would recommend monitoring to a patient predisposed to or living with a condition, and which conditions to target first.

Working with the panel and engineering, we co-created a patient journey for Medicare patients with chronic conditions—mapping when data collection, physician review, and intervention should happen across the care cycle.

Prototyping · Information architecture and data visualization

I designed an initial physician experience focused on information architecture, data visualization, and core requests from the panel: viewing medication and patient history in one place.

Early feedback was positive, with clear gaps to close. Doctors needed to contact patients directly, manage how long monitoring would run, and interact with charts that surfaced detail on demand—not static summaries.

I iterated through weekly panel sessions, tightening workflows for clinical decision-making while keeping the experience scannable during high-volume practice days.

Design

From a schedule-first tool to a monitoring-first morning briefing.

The existing physician app centered on daily logistics—appointments, patient lookup, and chart review. Remote monitoring data existed, but lived behind notifications with no way to act on exceptions inline. The redesign put threshold alerts where doctors already start their day.

Before · Schedule-centric workflow

  • Appointments — daily schedule
  • Patient detail — overview, history, prescriptions

After · Monitoring-first briefing

  • Overview — morning briefing with inline monitoring alerts instead of buried notifications
  • Patient detail — monitoring tab with trend charts, device context, and prescriptions

Results

User-centered design led to high-growth, high-margin recurring revenue.

  • Heal’s repeat patient rate increased to 47%, exceeding our 45% goal while reducing acute episodes and improving health outcomes
  • With monitoring paid by Medicare, the program was free for patients and drove recurring net margins of more than $4M per year
  • 39% reduction in unnecessary prescriptions for monitored patients
  • Monitoring capabilities supported Heal’s Medicare approval and later acquisition by Humana

This project showed how designing for physician visibility—not just patient-facing features—could unlock preventative care at home. By giving doctors a durable view into chronic conditions, we helped Heal move from on-demand visits to ongoing primary care.
