Turning a pass-through account into a financial home

Situation

Only 32% of sellers who elected Checking in Square onboarding activated.

Square scaled Checking enrollment through seller onboarding, and enrollment worked. Activation did not. Sellers completed identity verification, then treated Checking as a pass-through account, transferring every payout out to an external bank.

This was an ambiguous problem, because the gap was not awareness. Sellers knew Checking existed. The gap was banking adoption: nobody was using the debit card or the money movement levers that make an account real. The big open question was why.

The ask was pointed: fix it on the Square Point of Sale app, where about 80% of first sales take place.

Task

Separate the opinions from the evidence.

The cross-functional team had reasonable instincts, all of them discoverability plays: add a stronger Banking entry point to the Square Daily Sales report email, give Banking better placement in the navigation, create more surface area. My responsibility was to find out what activated sellers actually did, and design against that.

Action

The data had an answer: sellers who activated engaged the debit card first.

Funnel analysis with Data Science showed debit card engagement was the strongest activation signal we had. Then we looked at the design and found the mismatch:

  • The debit card was buried below the fold
  • The money movement levers sellers expected were also buried
  • There was no system status: nothing told a seller when their card would arrive, or that they could spend immediately

That last gap made Apple Wallet a key metric. With the virtual card added to Wallet, a seller could take their first sale and spend from it the same day. The reframe: stop making Banking more findable, start making the activating behaviors unmissable.

Design

Debit and money movement above the fold, with a system that talks back.

Square Point of Sale · Before and after

Scroll inside each device to view the full screen.

  • System status. A visible "We're printing your card" tracker replaced silence: arrival date for the physical card, and the message that the virtual card is ready now.
  • Spend immediately. Add to Apple Wallet surfaced at the moment of highest motivation, right after enrollment.
  • Above the fold. Deposit, Transfer, and Pay promoted to the top of the banking home. The behaviors that predict activation stopped hiding.
  • Ownership. Account and routing numbers one tap away, reinforcing that this is a real business bank account, not a feature.

Results

Behavior changed, not just dashboard numbers.

Onboarding-acquired seller activation went from 32% to over 40%. First card spend within a month rose from 29% to 51%. Apple Pay / Wallet adoption climbed from 18% to 44%.

Underneath the metrics: sellers began holding and spending balances inside Square instead of routing payouts out. Every change shipped behind an experiment with a pre-registered metric, measured against the 32% baseline.

Next phase

With activation fixed: actionable insights.

Fixing Checking earned the right to ask a bigger question: what should Square Banking become? Checking AI explorations were also being considered in the Cash Flow team's vision work sessions, where they helped inform a larger Banking vision. As part of that north star work I prototyped a money management hub with AI-powered insights, sequenced deliberately after the foundational behaviors this quarter proved out, because insights only matter to sellers whose money already lives here.

Reflection

Activation was a visibility problem wearing an awareness costume.

The room wanted to make Banking easier to find. The data showed activated sellers were already finding it; what they engaged with first was the debit card, and the design was hiding it. Trusting the funnel over the opinions is what moved the number.
